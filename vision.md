# Vision

## Multilingual Workforce Safety

Canada's construction workforce is multilingual.

Many skilled workers are highly competent in their trade but face safety risks when critical jobsite instructions are only available in English.

Torch should support language-independent knowledge access.

Workers should be able to ask Jack questions in their strongest language and receive clear, safety-conscious answers while preserving key jobsite terms in English.

Jack should help bridge language gaps without replacing site procedures, supervision, or required workplace communication standards.

Core principle:

A language barrier should not erase a worker's skill or put them in danger.
